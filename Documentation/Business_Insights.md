📊 Business Insights
Overall Churn Rate: The company has a churn rate of 26.54%, with 1,869 out of 7,043 customers leaving the service.

Contract Type Impact: Customers on Month-to-Month contracts have the highest churn rate (42.71%). Customers with One-Year and Two-Year contracts are significantly more likely to stay.

Internet Service: Fiber Optic customers show the highest churn rate (41.89%), indicating potential issues with pricing, service quality, or customer expectations.

Payment Method: Customers using Electronic Check have the highest churn rate (45.29%), suggesting this payment method is associated with higher customer attrition.

Monthly Charges: Customers who churn generally have higher average monthly charges, indicating that pricing may influence customer retention.

Senior Citizens: Senior citizens churn at a higher rate than non-senior customers, highlighting the need for targeted retention strategies.

Revenue Impact: Customer churn represents an estimated $2.86 million in lost revenue, emphasizing the financial importance of improving customer retention.

💡 Recommendations
Convert Month-to-Month customers to annual contracts using discounts and loyalty offers.

Improve the Fiber Optic customer experience by reviewing pricing, network quality, and customer support.

Encourage customers to switch from Electronic Check to automatic payment methods with cashback or incentives.

Offer personalized retention campaigns for customers with less than one year of tenure.

Promote value-added services such as Online Security and Tech Support to increase customer engagement and reduce churn.
